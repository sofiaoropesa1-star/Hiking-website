Introduction to Hiking

Hiking is exploring nature on foot: forests, mountains, canyons, and more. It builds fitness and reduces stress.

Begin with short trails, wear good shoes, and respect the environment.

Mountain Hiking

Expect steep climbs, rocky terrain, and cooler temps at elevation. Prepare for quick weather changes.

  • Layer clothing
  • Bring navigation & extra water
  • Wear supportive boots

Canyon Trails

Deep walls and narrow paths â€” canyons provide shade but may be prone to flash floods. Check forecasts.

  • Watch footing on loose rock
  • Carry sun protection for exposed areas
  • Avoid canyons during heavy rain

Forest Trails

Shady, peaceful, and full of life. Great for beginners and wildlife spotting.

  • Bring insect repellent
  • Stay on marked trails
  • Carry binoculars for birds

How to Prepare

Research, pack essentials, and let someone know your plan.

  • Map, phone, first-aid
  • Water, snacks, sun protection
  • Layer clothing and rain gear

Dangers of Hiking

Know the risks: weather, wildlife, dehydration, injuries, and lost trails.

  • Carry first-aid
  • Watch the weather
  • Bring extra water and food

What to Do If You Get Lost

Use STOP: Stop, Think, Observe, Plan. Stay visible and conserve resources.

  • Use whistle or mirror to signal
  • Stay put if rescue is likely
  • Only retrace steps if sure
