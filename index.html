Introduction to Hiking

Hiking is walking through natural places such as forests, mountains, and deserts. It’s a great way to explore nature while improving your fitness. Always plan your route and be aware of your surroundings to stay safe.

Quick Summary

  • Good exercise
  • Low cost to start
  • Bring water and good shoes

How to Prepare

Research the trail, check weather, pack water & snacks, and tell someone your plan. Bring a small first-aid kit and a map or GPS device to avoid getting lost. Dress in layers so you can adjust to changing temperatures and conditions.

Quick Summary

  • Check weather & route
  • Pack essentials
  • Start easy

If You Get Lost

Stay calm, use STOP (Stop, Think, Observe, Plan), and make yourself visible. Avoid wandering aimlessly, which can make it harder for rescuers to find you. Leave markers like stacked rocks or visible items to signal your path.

Quick Summary

  • Stop moving
  • Make yourself visible
  • Signal for help

Dangers of Hiking

Beware wildlife, terrain, dehydration, and sudden weather. Watch for slippery rocks or loose soil to prevent falls. Carry extra water and snacks to maintain energy and avoid fatigue during your hike.

Quick Summary

  • Weather changes fast
  • Stay hydrated
  • Watch your footing
