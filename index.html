  • Lots of trees

Canyon Hiking

  • Large cliffs
  • Dry air
  • Beautiful rock layers

Mountain Hiking

Mountain trails are steep, rocky, and challenging but offer breathtaking views.

  • High altitude
  • Steep climbs
  • Epic views

Research your trail for length, difficulty, and weather. Dress appropriately and layer your clothing. Pack water, snacks, first aid kit, and a light source.

Inform someone about your route and expected return. Start with easy trails to build experience.

Quick Summary

  • Research the trail.
  • Dress for the weather.
  • Pack essentials.
  • Tell someone your plan.
  • Start with easy trails.

Weather can change quickly, and wildlife encounters or dehydration may occur. Stay alert and follow safety rules.

Watch your footing to avoid slips and falls.

Quick Summary

  • Weather can change fast.
  • Watch for wildlife.

  • Drink water to prevent dehydration.
  • Watch your footing.

Introduction to Hiking

Hiking is walking through forests, mountains, deserts, and parks. Anyone can enjoy it without special skills.

It strengthens your legs, improves breathing, reduces stress, and allows you to connect with nature in a meaningful way.

Quick Summary

  • Walking in nature
  • Improves health and reduces stress
  • Anyone can start with basic gear
  • Simple outdoor activity
  • Can be short or long distance

How to Prepare

Good preparation includes researching your trail, checking weather, wearing layers, and carrying important items such as water, snacks, a phone, and basic first aid.

Quick Summary

  • Research the trail
  • Dress for weather
  • Carry essentials
  • Tell someone your plan
  • Start with easy trails

Dangers of Hiking

Nature can be unpredictable. Weather changes suddenly, animals may appear, dehydration can sneak up on you, and injuries like twisted ankles can occur if you're not careful.

Quick Summary

  • Weather changes quickly
  • Watch for wildlife
  • Stay hydrated
  • Stay on the trail
  • Be aware of surroundings

What to Do If You Get Lost

If you find yourself lost, follow the STOP method: Stop, Think, Observe, and Plan. Staying calm and staying in one place usually increases your chance of being found quickly.

Quick Summary

  • Stay calm
  • STOP method
  • Retrace steps only if safe
  • Make yourself visible
  • Signal for help
