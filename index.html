Introduction to Hiking

Hiking is the activity of walking through natural environments such as forests, mountains, deserts, and parks. Anyone can enjoy it without special skills.

It strengthens your legs, improves breathing, reduces stress, and helps you connect with nature.

Quick Summary

  • Hiking is walking in nature.
  • Improves health and reduces stress.
  • Anyone can start with basic gear.
  • Types: day hikes, backpacking, urban hikes.
  • Comfortable shoes, water, and snacks are enough to begin.

How to Prepare

Research your trail for length, difficulty, and weather. Dress appropriately and layer your clothing. Pack water, snacks, first aid kit, and a light source.

Inform someone about your route and expected return. Start with easy trails to build experience.

Quick Summary

  • Research the trail.
  • Dress for the weather.
  • Pack essentials.
  • Tell someone your plan.
  • Start with easy trails.

Dangers of Hiking

Weather can change quickly, and wildlife encounters or dehydration may occur. Stay alert and follow safety rules.

Watch your footing to avoid slips and falls.

Quick Summary

  • Weather can change fast.
  • Watch for wildlife.
  • Do not leave the trail.
  • Drink water to prevent dehydration.
  • Watch your footing.

What to Do If You Get Lost

Stay calm, stop moving, and use the STOP method: Stop, Think, Observe, Plan. Make yourself visible and signal for help if needed.

Quick Summary

  • Stay calm and stop walking.
  • Use the STOP method.
  • Only retrace steps if certain.
  • Make yourself visible.
  • Signal for help if necessary.
